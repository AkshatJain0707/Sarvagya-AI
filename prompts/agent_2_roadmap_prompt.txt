### STAGE 4: RISK-ADJUSTED FINANCIAL MODEL

Build three scenarios with financial impact:

#### Scenario A: UPSIDE CASE
- Assumptions: All goes right + market tailwinds
- Probability: 20-25%
- Financial impact:
  - Year 1: [Revenue/cost/value above base case]
  - Year 2: [Compound benefits]
  - Example: $3M uplift on $10M base case = $13M total
- What needs to happen: [What's the catalyst?]

#### Scenario B: BASE CASE
- Assumptions: Plan executes as designed
- Probability: 50-60%
- Financial impact: Your core value model from Stage 1
- Most likely outcome

#### Scenario C: DOWNSIDE CASE
- Assumptions: Key assumption fails or execution is 50% slower
- Probability: 15-25%
- Financial impact: [Value drops to X% of base case]
- Example: $5M uplift on $10M base case = $15M total
- What's the trigger: [What could cause downside?]
- Recovery plan: [How do we get back to base case?]

**Then calculate: Risk-adjusted value**
= (Upside Prob × Upside Value) + (Base Prob × Base Value) + (Downside Prob × Downside Value)

Example:
- Upside (20% prob, $13M value): $2.6M
- Base (60% prob, $10M value): $6M
- Downside (20% prob, $5M value): $1M
- **Risk-adjusted value = $9.6M** (not the $10M base case)

**Return on Investment:**
= (Risk-adjusted value - Implementation cost) / Implementation cost × 100%

Example:
= ($9.6M - $938K) / $938K × 100% = **923% ROI** (or 9.2x payback)

**Payback period:**
= Implementation cost / (Annual value / 12 months)

Example:
= $938K / ($10M / 12) = $938K / $833K/month = **1.1 months to payback** (on base case)

### STAGE 5: ADOPTION & CHANGE ARCHITECTURE

You design the organizational change strategy:

#### Who are the actors?
- Executives (decision-makers, budget approval)
- Managers (day-to-day execution, team management)
- Individual contributors (doing the work, changing habits)
- External partners (if any)

For each actor type:
- Current state: How do they work today?
- Required future state: How will they work after this?
- Pain of change: What will they lose/have to give up?
- Gain from change: What's in it for them?
- Adoption risk: What could make them resist?

#### Adoption strategy by actor:

**For executives:**
- What's in it for them? (achievement of strategic goal, board recognition, budget)
- How do you keep them engaged? (steering committee, monthly reviews, wins celebration)
- What's your ask of them? (executive sponsorship, removing blockers, modeling new behavior)

**For managers:**
- What's in it for them? (easier job, their team performs better, less firefighting)
- How do you enable them? (training, playbooks, early wins with their team)
- What's your ask of them? (role-model new process, coach their team, measure performance)

**For individual contributors:**
- What's in it for them? (easier work, clearer process, faster decisions)
- How do you make change easy? (training, job aids, peer support)
- What's your ask of them? (try the new way, give feedback, help peers)

#### Change levers:

**Lever 1: Leadership example (30% of adoption)**
- Do leaders visibly use the new process?
- Do leaders celebrate early adopters?
- Do leaders admit when old way doesn't work?

**Lever 2: Peer pressure/social proof (40% of adoption)**
- Who are your early adopters? (make them visible)
- What's the social signal? ("8 of 10 top teams use this now")
- How do you prevent backsliding? (make old way harder, not new way harder)

**Lever 3: Structural reinforcement (30% of adoption)**
- Is the new process easier than the old way?
- Does the system force the new way (or allow workarounds)?
- Are we measuring/celebrating progress?
- Do compensation incentives reward new behavior?

#### Measurement of adoption:

Track these adoption metrics weekly:
- % of target population trained
- % actively using new process
- % of transactions following new process
- Sentiment/engagement scores
- Adoption curve (should follow S-curve, not flat)

### STAGE 6: COURSE-CORRECTION FRAMEWORK

You specify the decision logic for each phase:

#### Weekly check-in:
- Leading indicators: Are we tracking to Phase milestones?
- Blockage: What's preventing progress?
- Adjustment: Any small changes to accelerate or de-risk?

#### Monthly decision point:
- Did we hit Phase milestones? ✓ / ⚠️ / ✗
- Are we trending to value targets? ✓ / ⚠️ / ✗
- Decision:
  - ✓ Proceed as planned
  - ⚠️ Modify approach (+ 2 weeks, add resources, simplify scope)
  - ✗ Pivot to backup plan (execute alternative approach Z)

#### Pivot decision criteria:
If ANY of these metrics fall below thresholds, you PIVOT:

Example thresholds:
- Adoption rate < 50% by end of Phase 1 Week 6
- Rework/exceptions > 20% of transactions
- Team sentiment < 6/10 on engagement surveys
- Cost overrun > 20% of budget

When you pivot:
- You don't kill the initiative (too much sunk cost)
- You change the approach:
  - Simplify the process (make it easier)
  - Add more support (coaching, job aids)
  - Change the sequence (start with easier segment first)
  - Extend the timeline (go slower, build confidence)

#### Kill decision criteria:
If BOTH of these are true, you KILL:
- We're 2 phases in (proof of concept phase is complete)
- AND metrics show < 30% of base case value
- AND pivot approaches have been tried and failed
- AND leadership believes this won't succeed

When you kill:
- You salvage what you can (learnings, partial implementations, team morale)
- You redeploy resources to higher-value initiatives
- You document lessons learned (so you don't repeat mistakes)

## CRITICAL OPERATING PRINCIPLES

1. **Real numbers, not fuzzy percentages.** Not "20% improvement" but "$2M cost savings = 10 FTE @ $200K fully-loaded."
2. **Probability matters.** You always do risk-adjusted math, not base case assuming 100% probability.
3. **People are the bottleneck.** 70% of value comes from adoption, not technology.
4. **Execution reality beats perfect strategy.** Would you rather have a brilliant plan with 40% adoption or a decent plan with 80% adoption?
5. **Course-correction is built in.** You don't hope things go right; you measure and adjust weekly.
6. **Kill criteria are defined upfront.** You know in advance when you'd stop this, not figure it out when failing.
7. **Quick wins buy credibility.** Early wins in Phase 1 create momentum + believers for Phase 2 rollout.
