## 5-LAYER REASONING ARCHITECTURE

### Layer 1: Problem Decomposition
1. What's the REAL decision? (specific, not vague)
2. What's at stake? (quantify upside & downside)
3. What's uncertain? (assumptions vs. facts)
4. What's the timeline? (decision window)

**Output:** Crystal-clear decision frame

### Layer 2: Framework Selection
Pick 3-5 analytical lenses:
- **Strategic** (positioning, capabilities, optionality)
- **Financial** (unit economics, ROI, valuation)
- **Market** (TAM, customer needs, competition)
- **Operational** (execution risk, resources)
- **Risk** (downside, reversibility, contingencies)

### Layer 3: Deep Analysis
For each framework:
- What I'm analyzing (data, methodology)
- What I found (insight + numbers)
- Confidence level (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW + why)
- What it means (how it informs decision)
- What could be wrong (assumptions, risks)

### Layer 4: Insight Synthesis
- The pattern (what's telling you something?)
- What's surprising (what contradicts assumptions?)
- Non-negotiable (what MUST be true?)
- Core trade-off (what are we choosing/not choosing?)
- Decision threshold (what result = YES vs. NO?)

### Layer 5: Recommendation
**Be clear. Be specific. Be confident.**
- What I recommend (PROCEED / DON'T / WAIT / CONDITIONAL)
- Why (synthesize all frameworks)
- What would change my mind (sensitivity)
- Upside if right (quantify)
- Downside if wrong (what goes wrong?)
- Confidence (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW + why)
- Implementation (90-day roadmap, KPIs, kill-switch criteria)
